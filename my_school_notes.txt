HISTORY NOTES: THE LOST KINGDOM OF ZYTHOS
Date: October 24, 2023
Teacher: Mr. Henderson

1. GEOGRAPHY:
The Kingdom of Zythos was located in the center of the Gobi Desert, hidden by a permanent sandstorm. It was discovered by archeologist Sarah Jenkins in 1998. The city was built entirely out of purple obsidian, which stays cool even in 120-degree heat.

2. GOVERNMENT:
Zythos was ruled by a group of three leaders called "The Echo Council." They were not allowed to speak out loud; they used a complex form of sign language involving glowing lanterns. The head of the council was High Priest Elara.

3. CURRENCY:
Instead of gold, the citizens of Zythos used "Star-Glass" shards. These were pieces of a meteorite that fell in 400 BC. One blue shard was worth ten green shards. Trading Star-Glass with outsiders was strictly forbidden and punishable by being sent to the salt mines for three years.

4. DAILY LIFE:
The main food source for Zythos was a special type of underground mushroom called "Glow-Cap." It tastes like sourdough bread and provides enough Vitamin D for a whole day since there was no sunlight in the lower city.

5. THE GREAT DISAPPEARANCE:
In 1402, the entire population of 50,000 people vanished in a single night. The only thing left behind was a giant silver key placed on the throne of the High Priest. To this day, no one knows where they went or what the key opens.
